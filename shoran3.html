Sarah joined CHSR in July 2011 as a graduate assistant and is now working full time on the Evaluation of CPS/DV Collocation project. The project is designed to evaluate a collaborative approach for serving families in New York State who are experiencing both intimate partner violence and child maltreatment. Prior to joining CHSR, Sarah worked throughout the public sector with the New York State Senate, the Office of the Attorney General, the Office of Children and Family Services Division of Child Care Services, and the Department of Residential Life at the University at Albany, SUNY. Sarah is also an Alumna of the Center for Women in Government and Civil Society’s Graduate Fellowship program.

Sarah received her Bachelor’s degree from the University at Albany, SUNY and her Master’s of Public Administration from the Rockefeller College of Public Affairs and Policy at the University at Albany, SUNY.
