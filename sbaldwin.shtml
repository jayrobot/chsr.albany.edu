Sean assists with the Victim Legal Assistance Network Development project, collecting data on crime and criminal justice in each county in New York State. He reviews current victim services practices, and studies trends in services and restitution for victims to predict future administrative practices. He performs data entry for the OPWDD project.

Sean earned a Bachelor’s degree in Political Science with a minor in Creative Arts from Siena College. He is currently pursuing a Master in Public Administration with concentrations in Social Welfare Policy and Politics, Policy, and Institutions, from SUNY Albany’s Rockefeller College of Public Affairs and Policy. Before working with CHSR, he was a graduate assistant in the Rockefeller College Office of Career Services. He currently serves as Secretary of the Public Affairs Student Association.
