CHSR receives OJJDP grant of $497,095 to evaluate parent engagement initiative

The Center for Human Services Research was awarded a $497,095 grant from the US Department of Justice, Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention, to study the effects of parent involvement on mentoring outcomes. CHSR is partnering with Big Brothers/Big Sisters of the Capital Region to conduct a quasi-experimental study that will offer insight into how parent engagement impacts on  increasing parental support of mentoring, enhancing the mentoring relationship (quality, intensity and duration) and reducing the precursors to juvenile delinquency.
