Lynn Warner's research focuses on inequities in mental health and substance abuse service delivery to vulnerable populations such as children, racial-ethnic minorities and low-income women.  Her research has been supported by the Annie E. Casey Foundation and the Alcoholic Beverage Medical Research Foundation.  She provides reviews for several social work and interdisciplinary journals and is a member of the editorial board of Social Work and Mental Health. Her experience includes management of a range of research and evaluation projects, including a national study of the connection between drug use and homicides, and a longitudinal intervention study of self-help group participation for persons with mental illness.  At CHSR, Dr. Warner has facilitated expanding the Center’s portfolio in the areas of children’s mental health services and health care for rural veterans.  At the School of Social Welfare, she teaches research and policy analysis courses.  She is also a faculty affiliate of the University at Albany’s Center for Women in Government & Civil Society.

Dr. Warner received a dual PhD in sociology and social work from the University of Michigan, an MSW also from the University of Michigan, and an MPP from the Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University.  She received a BA from Smith College.  She obtained training in psychiatric epidemiology and quantitative methods as a National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) pre-doctoral fellow at the Institute for Social Research (University of Michigan) and in services research as an NIMH post-doctoral research fellow at the Institute for Health, Health Care Policy and Aging Research (Rutgers University).
