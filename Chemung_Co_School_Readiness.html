Chemung County School Readiness Project

The Chemung County School Readiness Project (SRP) is a grass–roots community collaborative aimed at improving children’s overall well-being and readiness for school.

Since 2006, the SRP has provided comprehensive services to Chemung County’s children from birth to 5 years old. The primary mission of the SRP is to enable children to enter kindergarten academically, socially and physically ready to learn.

Activities associated with the Chemung County SRP fall into five categories:

  • Nurse Home Visits
  • Quality Child Care
  • Parent Education
  • Health Care
  • Community Coordination and Communication

A baseline study was completed in partnership with the Teacher’s College at Columbia University. Findings present a comprehensive school readiness profile of 2007-08 entering kindergartners, and suggest that 53% of Chemung County kindergartners were not ready to enter school. The evaluation plan also includes a comprehensive follow-up study of the 2011-12 kindergarten cohort to determine if SRP interventions impacted school readiness levels. Data collection is generally conducted during the school registration process, and includes the following measures:

  • Teacher Child Ratings System (TCRS)
  • Child Observation Record (COR)
  • Parent Assessment of Child Experiences (PACE)

Evaluation & Research

The SRP will complete 5 years of service delivery in 2011. As directed by the project Steering Committee and Council, CHSR will design and conduct an evaluation that measures overall system effectiveness and explores differential impacts of specific services on specific populations of children and families. Data analyses will focus on comparing the 2007-08 (baseline) and 2011-12 (post intervention) kindergarten classes on comprehensive measures of school readiness. Available parent and provider survey data will also be analyzed to explore multiple dimensions of system efficacy. To measure the impact of early childhood services offered through the Chemung County SRP, data collection and analysis protocols will be as consistent as possible across time. However, some alterations and additions may be suggested based on lessons learned from the baseline evaluation. Data collection will primarily take place during the Fall of 2011. CHSR will review all baseline data and meet with SRP leaders and stakeholders to collaboratively devise evaluation and data collection strategies.

Alterations to the baseline data collection protocol may include:

  1. Improving the breadth and clarity of parent and provider surveys
  2. Working with school districts to obtain student achievement data
  3. Conducting brief cognitive assessments with children
