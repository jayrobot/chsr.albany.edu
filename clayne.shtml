China Layne is a member of the research team for the Healthy Families New York program.  Her current projects include work on the randomized control trial evaluation, the early enrollment pilot project, and the fatherhood advocacy initiative.  Before joining CHSR, China worked as a research analyst in the areas of education, workforce development, and economic development in New York, Massachusetts, and New Hampshire.

China received a BA in Philosophy and the History of Science and Mathematics from St. John’s College, an MA in Regional Economic and Social Development from University of Massachusetts at Lowell, and a PhD in Sociology from University at Albany.  Her dissertation examined race and gender differences among over-educated workers and the role of labor market changes in contributing to the increase in over-education in recent decades.
