Frequently Asked Questions


What does CHSR do?
CHSR conducts program evaluation of human and social service programs in the capital region and greater New York area, providing data analysis of evaluation and informing program and policy decisions that surround children, families, and community organizations. CHSR has an IT component that develps systems to managage all the data coming in, so that it is easily accessible for future analysis; CHSR also develops customized management information systems for human service programs in the field.

How is CHSR funded?
CHSR is funded entirely by external contracts.  We have contracts with various New York State Agencies, City of Albany School District, Orange County (NYS), Prevent Child Abuse New Jersey to name a few.

What is CHSR’s affiliation with the University at Albany?
CHSR is physically located on the University at Albany’s downtown campus.   We have access to the University Libraries, a vast network of University researcher’s and specialists, certain University resources, and employ undergraduate and graduate students during the year.

What is CHSR’s affiliation with the School of Social Welfare?
Organizationally, CHSR is operates within the School of Social Welfare. Dr. Lynn Warner, Co-Director of CHSR is a associate professor in the School of Social Welfare.

What does the IT/MIS Department do?
We provide customized computer information solutions, specializing in human service organizations. We have grown from a small university-based IT support unit to a team of professionals designing applications for a broad range of community-based organizations. Our IT experts have experience in direct service delivery, which enhances our understanding and commitment to human service agencies. We have a deep understanding of family dynamics and how to build applications to capture these complexities.

What are CHSR’s Facilities and Administrative (indirect) costs?
Though the standard F&A rate, formerly known as indirect costs, is set by the Federal Government at 51%, there is latitude to get that reduced to a reasonable amount for our partners.

What does the Facilities and Administrative cost rate cover?
Formerly known as indirect costs, these are expenses associated with the use of University facilities and various administrative costs that cannot be directly and uniquely assigned to any particular project. Examples of F&A costs include administrative support, workspace, maintenance and utilities.
