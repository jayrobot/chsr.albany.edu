Nicole Neu-Baker started with CHSR in fall 2019 as a Research Scientist, primarily providing technical writing support for various CHSR projects, including the Preschool Development Grant Birth Through Five project. Since 2012, Nicole also works as a Research Associate for NanoHealth Initiatives at SUNY Polytechnic Institute, College of Nanoscale Science & Engineering. She brings expertise in environmental, occupational, and public health as well as significant technical and grant writing experience to CHSR. Her public health research experience includes occupational health and safety assessments for the nanotech workforce as well as investigating neurotoxic effects of environmental pollutants across the lifespan and associated risk for cognitive disorders.

Nicole earned her Bachelor’s degree in Psychology and Neuroscience at Ithaca College and a Master’s degree in Public Health from the University at Albany (SUNY) School of Public Health.
