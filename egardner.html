Erin is currently a research assistant at CHSR where she primarily works on a training evaluation of Healthy Families New York along with many other aspects of HFNY. Erin has worked in a variety of research settings most of which focused on promoting positive development among at-risk youth, parenting styles and attachment, and the effects of stigma on one’s self-esteem. She has experience with qualitative and quantitative data collection and analysis methods along with research design and implementation. Further, she has worked for a variety of residential treatment facilities for at-risk youth and also at the Stony Brook University Career center while receiving her Master’s in Psychology.

Erin attended University at Albany for her Bachelor’s in Psychology and has now returned to receive her Masters and Ph.D. in Social Work.
