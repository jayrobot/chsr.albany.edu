Adirondack Rural Health Network Logic Model Development and Technical Assistance Project

 

CHSR is workingCHSR is working with the Adirondack Rural Health Network to assist mini-grant recipients develop logic models and evaluation plans for their local projects to increase physical activity and access to nutritious foods in the Adirondack region. CHSR is also providing evaluation consultation to the Community Health Planning Committee as they embark upon a strategic planning and workplan development initiative.

Through the Adirondack Rural Health Network Community Health Planning Committee, mini-grants up to $5,000 were made available to support efforts to increase physical activity and access to nutritious foods in the Adirondack region. Funding is meant to further the objective of the ARHN’s Community Health Planning Committee’s selected priority area for 2010-2014 and to bring the region closer to the NYS Department of Health’s Prevention Agenda goals for physical activity and nutrition. Six projects were funded that addressed one of four key interest areas: community gardens, complete streets, school- community joint use policies, and provider involvement in obesity prevention.

CHSR developed logic models for each of the six mini-grant projects, followed by individualized technical assistance with project directors. CHSR is also assisting the Community Health Planning Committee on logic modeling and evaluation design as part of its overall strategic planning process.
